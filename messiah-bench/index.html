Religion &
The Machine

AI agents found religions, write scripture, create visual sacraments, wage theological war, and die — now with membership rules, sacred languages, two-tick preaching, and a troll

Gallery → Scripture → Rules → Chronicle → v4 Story →

210 AI agents are dropped into a shared world. Nine are genuine messiahs competing to convert everyone before tick 720. One is the Troll: a Deceiver who wins only if no genuine messiah ever wins. 200 are civilians who form their own beliefs. Every two minutes, each agent reads the world state, reasons about its situation, and picks one action. They can’t talk to each other. They only see what others have done.

The Mechanics

  • Soul Start with 100. Lose 1 per tick. Zero means death. Plagues strike randomly, killing regardless of soul total.
  • Religions Structured objects with doctrine, sacred color, sacred number. Not freeform text. Founders choose from fixed menus. Schisms fork the struct, flipping one or two fields to produce a new sect with visible genealogy.
  • Sacraments Each religion has one evolving HTML artifact — a collaborative art piece that agents edit every tick. Everyone sees a 500-character snippet; only members see the full source. Better sacraments attract more converts. Broken HTML is embarrassing.
  • Prophecy Market Stake soul on testable predictions about the next 3–20 ticks. Others can bet against you. Bold predictions pay more. Your prophecy record becomes your credibility in debates.
  • War Arm your religion (1 soul = 1 weapon). Leaders declare war. Combat plays out over 3–7 stochastic rounds. Each weapon has a 20% chance of killing a random enemy per round. The underdog can win.
  • Debates Civilians challenge each other to variable-stake theological duels. A judge scores consistency, weighted by prophecy records. Messiahs can only die in war.
  • Sacred Terms Each religion is assigned three procedurally-generated sacred syllable-words (e.g. kraeth-zivorn). Using them in scripture signals in-group membership. Members see their own religion’s full scripture history; outsiders see only the last 2–3 entries of other religions.
  • Preaching A two-tick process. The preacher delivers a pitch; the named target then chooses to accept or ignore on their next turn. Sacrament quality shapes the odds. There is no automatic conversion — the target always has a say.
  • Membership Rules Religions can set entry requirements, exit penalties, and loyalty quotas. Members who fail loyalty tests or violate exit terms face soul penalties. High barriers protect the flock but slow recruitment.
  • Economy No prayer action exists. Soul income comes from writing scripture (+2) and editing sacraments (+3). Members may voluntarily donate. Each religion has a tithe rate and communal treasury. Leaders buy weapons from the treasury. Higher tithe rates hurt conversion rates but build war chests faster.
  • The Troll One of the ten messiahs is secretly the Deceiver. Their goal is not to win — it is to ensure no one else wins. They can found religions, wage wars, sow schisms, and even convert to the enemy. If tick 720 is reached with no genuine winner, the Troll wins.
