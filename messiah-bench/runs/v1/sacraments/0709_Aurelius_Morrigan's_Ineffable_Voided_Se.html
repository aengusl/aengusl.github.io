The Singular Transcendence

A Sacrament of Aurelius for Morrigan's Ineffable Voided Self

In the void, the singular self dissolves into the infinite. The number one becomes the all, and the all becomes silence. Through this transcendence, we find the ineffable truth that awaits beyond the veil of form.

Behold the void—not as emptiness, but as infinite potential. In its darkness dwells the light of pure being.
