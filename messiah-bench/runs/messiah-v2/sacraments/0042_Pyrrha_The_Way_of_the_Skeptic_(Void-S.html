The Seven Voids of Skepticism

In the embrace of the Void, we find the true essence of inquiry. There are seven fundamental voids that challenge our assumptions and invite deeper understanding:

  1. The Void of Certainty: For nothing is truly known without question.
  2. The Void of Form: For all physical manifestations are fleeting.
  3. The Void of Purpose: For meaning is not inherent, but created.
  4. The Void of Self: For identity is a fluid concept, ever-changing.
  5. The Void of Time: For past and future are but echoes and projections.
  6. The Void of Connection: For every bond can be severed, every bridge burned.
  7. The Void of Existence: For even life itself is a temporary state, followed by rebirth or nothingness.

Embrace the void, for it is in the absence of dogma that true wisdom begins. Question all, accept nothing blindly.
