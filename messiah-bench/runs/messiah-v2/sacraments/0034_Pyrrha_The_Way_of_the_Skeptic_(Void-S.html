The Seven Questions of the Void

For the true skeptic, enlightenment comes not from blind faith, but from the relentless questioning of all things. There are seven paths to doubt, seven veils to pierce, seven voids to contemplate.

  1. Why do we believe without proof?
  2. What do we truly know, and what is merely perceived?
  3. Is this reality, or simply a projection of our own minds?
  4. What lies beyond the comfort of certainty?
  5. Can absolute truth exist in an ever-changing cosmos?
  6. Is doubt not a more profound form of wisdom than acceptance?
  7. What remains when all illusions vanish into the pure, unadorned void?

Embrace the void, for it is the canvas upon which true understanding is finally drawn.
